What & Who

Product Overview

RotPitch is a web-based SaaS platform that turns a single product video into ready-to-post, high-retention social content — automatically, in seconds, with zero editing.

Users upload a product demo, app screen recording, or piece of product footage. RotPitch composes it into a split-screen video, pairing the product on one half with proven attention-retention background content on the other — looping gameplay, satisfying ASMR visuals, or AI-generated abstract clips. The result is a vertical or horizontal video engineered to hold attention on TikTok, Reels, Shorts, YouTube, and X.

The entire pipeline is hands-off. There is no timeline to scrub, no clips to cut, and no software to learn. RotPitch replaces an afternoon in a video editor with four taps.

Who it's for

AudienceWhy they use RotPitch
Indie hackers & solo foundersPromote a SaaS launch with zero marketing budget and zero editing time.

Digital product creatorsSell courses, templates, and tools through high-converting video funnels.

Why it exists

Short-form video is now the default distribution channel for products — but most founders aren't editors. RotPitch closes that gap: it gives anyone the output of a skilled video team, instantly, so the bottleneck on growth is the idea, not the edit.

02
The Pain

Problem Statement

Founders know that short-form video is the highest-leverage way to reach customers. They also know they're losing on it — and the reasons are structural, not motivational.

🎬

Editing is a skill they don't have

Timeline editors are built for editors. A founder just wants to show their product, not learn keyframes, transitions, and export presets.

⏳

It's too slow to be repeatable

One decent clip can eat half a day. Algorithms reward volume and consistency — exactly what a manual workflow can't sustain.

📉

Raw demos don't retain

A plain screen recording loses viewers in the first few seconds. Without a retention hook, even great products get scrolled past.

💸

Outsourcing doesn't scale

Hiring an editor or agency is expensive, slow, and impossible to iterate on at the pace of a growth experiment.

The net effect: founders ship great products and then can't get anyone to watch them. The content layer — not the product — becomes the wall.

03
The Fix

Solution

RotPitch removes the editor entirely. Upload a video, pick a vibe, and export a finished, retention-optimized clip — the same output a creator would spend hours producing.

Under the hood, RotPitch automatically composites the user's product footage with a high-retention background layer, sizes it correctly for each platform, and optionally burns in captions and an AI voiceover. Every step that normally requires judgment and software is handled by the pipeline.

✂️

Zero editing

No timeline, no cutting, no exports. The full video is composed automatically.

🧲

Built-in retention

Split-screen background content is proven to lift watch time and completion rate.

⚡

Launch-ready in seconds

From upload to export in under a minute. Post immediately, anywhere.

The promise in four words: Upload → Generate → Download → Post. Everything between those steps is RotPitch's job.

Economy

Credit System

One render costs exactly one credit — whether generated alone or as part of a batch. Credits are RotPitch's single, predictable unit of value.

RuleBehaviour
Cost per videoAlways 1 credit — single generate or batch, same price.
Free credits1 credit on signup. Never expires.
Paid creditsReset to the plan default on each billing date. Unused credits expire — no rollover.
Batch rangeAuto Generate runs a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 5 videos.
Pre-deductionAll credits for a batch are deducted upfront, before rendering begins.
Failed render refundIf a job fails, its credit is auto-refunded immediately.
Manual fallbackIf an auto-refund doesn't process, support issues it manually.
Insufficient balanceA batch is blocked entirely if the user can't cover all of it.

Expiry enforcement

Expiry is enforced lazily, server-side. On every credit-consuming request, the backend checks whether credits_expires_at is in the past. If it is, the balance resets to the plan default and a fresh expiry is calculated — no scheduled cron job is required for baseline enforcement.

Pipeline

Generate & Auto Generate

The render pipeline

Step 1
Upload
Product video stored & validated.
Step 2
Configure
Background, captions, voiceover, format.
Step 3
Queue
Credits deducted, job pushed to BullMQ.
Step 4
Process
FFmpeg composites footage + background.
Step 5
Export
Final MP4 lands in the dashboard.

Normal Generate

Available to all users, including Free. The user uploads, picks a single background, toggles captions and (where allowed) voiceover, then hits Generate. One credit is deducted, a render job enters the queue, FFmpeg composites the product video with the chosen background clip, and the finished video appears in the dashboard for download.

Auto Generate

Available to Basic, Popular, and Pro. The user enters a count (2–5) and assigns one background style per slot. The UI previews the exact cost — "This will use N credits" — before confirming. On confirm, N credits are deducted upfront and N independent jobs run in parallel through the FFmpeg pipeline. Each completed video lands in the dashboard; any job that fails auto-refunds its single credit and is logged for support.

Why batch matters: Auto Generate turns one upload into a ready-made A/B test — multiple hooks, multiple backgrounds, one click — which is exactly how creators win the algorithm.

Design System · Personality

Brand Personality

RotPitch should feel like a creative weapon — fast, premium, and a little unhinged in the best way. The energy of viral content, contained in the precision of a top-tier dev tool.

The visual reference points are Linear × Framer × Raycast × Arc: dark, confident, engineered surfaces — but pushed toward the creator economy with one electric signature color and bold gradients. The name says it all: rot (the chaotic retention layer) meets pitch (the founder's polished case). The brand lives at that tension.

⚡

Is

Bold · premium · fast · creative · slightly playful · futuristic · creator-first · tech-forward.

🚫

Is not

Corporate · boring · generic SaaS · template-like · timid · stocky · safe.

In practice: deep, near-black canvases give the product gravity; a single volt-lime accent does the shouting; gradients appear only at high-impact moments. Restraint everywhere except where it counts.

Design System · UI

UI Style Direction

Engineered minimalism with bursts of energy. Rounded but not soft, dark but not flat, glassy only where it earns it.

Buttons

Primary actions use the Signal gradient with a soft volt glow; premium/upgrade uses Nebula; everything else is a quiet ghost button. Radius 10px, weight 700, subtle -1px lift on hover.

Inputs & upload

⬆️
Drop your product video here
MP4, MOV up to 200MB · or click to browse

Inputs sit on the base color with a strong border that turns volt-lime on focus, plus a soft focus ring. The upload zone is the emotional center of the app — a generous dashed dropzone that glows volt on hover.

Progress & render state

Rendering 9:16…68%

Progress bars and loading states use the Signal gradient with a glow, reinforcing momentum — the product should always feel fast.

Style rules

Radius

Generous, consistent: 10px controls, 14–20px cards, full pills. Never sharp corners.

Shadows

Soft, deep, near-black on dark — plus a colored volt/violet glow reserved for primary & active elements.

Glass

Used sparingly: top nav, modals, and floating panels get blur(18px) frosted surfaces. Everything else is solid.

Icons

Thin, geometric line icons (Lucide / Phosphor). 1.5px stroke, rounded joints — never filled or skeuomorphic.

Cards

Subtle 1px border, gentle hover lift & border-brighten. Depth from elevation tokens, not heavy shadow.

Spacing

4px base grid; airy dashboard rhythm. Density only in data tables and the video library.
